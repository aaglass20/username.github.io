Group 1 - Defend the Goal

  • 5-6 Balls
  • Player is making sure coach does not score
  • Work on shuffling body to ball opposed to raising foot
  • Defending and keeping arms down
  • Work Left, right, Up, down
  • Be urgent, get rid of ball quick
  • Play to teammate
  • Not shown in vid, but teammate could work turning the ball out
Why is this important?
  • Stresses urgency of getting to ball in danger zone
  • Works moving body opposed to limbs
  • Works trapping on all levels, low, high, medium
  • Keeps mind set of going outside, playing to a teammte or zone

Group 2 - Fire Drill, Clear quickly, danger zone

  • 2-3 players in front of goal
  • Other players on outside shagging balls, bringing back to coach
  • Coach can lob ball in, put in on the ground, give variety
  • Rapidly play in 4-5 balls
  • Players call and clear. Own it if you say you have it. (If two call both are trying to clear)
  • Point is, you say you got it, you get it
  • Others should be aware that the closest teamate is the one that should be calling and owning it
  • Rotate after balls are played
Why is this important?
  • Not thinking, clearing in danger
  • Communicate
  • Knowing your zones, in goal box is danger

Group 3 - Give and Go, eyes on ball

  • Two lines
  • Player starts with ball, communicates who passing to
  • Player that pass takes off
  • Player receiving the ball says name or "Go" and passes on fly
  • Player receiving ball shoots
  • Both players touch cones on Goal box
  • Run backwards keeping eyes on Ball
  • Coach throws ball at towards players, player nearest calls and traps
Why is this important?
  • Working on Pass and move
  • Communications
  • Eyes on ball
  • Continue on trapping skills

Good Pass, Play O, Bad Pass Play D - Transitions

  • Goal throws ball to first player
  • Player attempts to pass through gate
  • If pass goes through gate, take off an become an option for leading pass
  • Try to pass before next gate to teammate that should be moving after the pass
  • If Player misses first gate, Second Player becomes offence
  • First player immediately had to transition from Offense to Defense
  • P2 is trying to score on keeper
  • P1 is trying to defend, not waiting on it
Why is this important?
  • P1 is transition to D to O if they make a good pass
  • P1 is transitioning quickly back to D if they make a bad pass
  • Promotes movement on O side if successful
  • There is a consequence for a bad pass
